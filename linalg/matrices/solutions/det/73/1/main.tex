From the given information, 
\begin{align}
\vec{A} = \myvec{1\\0 },
\vec{B} = \myvec{6\\0 },
\vec{C} = \myvec{4\\ 3\\}
\end{align}
Here,\\
Draw a perpendicular line from C to AB.
\begin{align}
    \vec{P} = \myvec{4\\0 }
    \label{det/73/1/eq}
\end{align}
Then Line CP becomes the height of the triangle\\
\\
The direction vectors of $AB$ and $PC$ are 
\begin{align}
\label{det/73/1/eq}
\vec{B}-\vec{A} &= \myvec{5\\0}  
\\
\vec{C}-\vec{P} &= \myvec{0\\3} 
\label{det/73/1/eq}
\end{align}
\begin{align}
\vec{M} = \myvec{\vec{B}-\vec{A} & \vec{C}-\vec{P}}^T 
\label{det/73/1/eq}
\end{align}
\begin{center}
\vec{M} = \begin{bmatrix}
 5 & 0 \\ 
    0 & 3 }\end{bmatrix}\\
 The area of $\triangle ABC$ is  obtained  in terms of the   magnitude of the determinant of the matrix $\vec{M}$ in  
\begin{align}
\Delta =\frac{1}{2}\mydet{\vec{M}}
\end{align}
\begin{equation} 
\label{det/73/1/eq}
\Delta = \frac12 \times  \begin{vmatrix}
 5 & 0 \\ 
    0 & 3 }
\end{vmatrix}    
    \label{det/73/1/eq}
\end{equation}
\Delta=7.5
